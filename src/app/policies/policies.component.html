TACTICAL APPROACH

2 key challenges holding development back for any geo-location in Zimbabwe are; 1) Clean Water and Sanitation 2) Affordable Energy (while these come with various forms and extensions) C4D contends that all other 15 SDGs will be achievable whenever availability of water and energy is guaranteed. We will partner investors, private companies, multi//bi lateral institutions, government, commercial entities, NGOs/CSOs/CBOs and progressive socio-economic formations, borehole drillers, energy producers and installers from either in or outside the country. Together with local citizens led by C4D Technocrats will design business projects that deliver win-win arrangements for investment returns whilst communities transform. C4D will PILOT model village, mining compounds, urban or peri-urban locations to test the best practices before replication. C4D will commission Feasibility Studies and Assessments of at least 8 sites that includes 2 rural, 2 mining areas, 2 peri urban and 2 urban centers whose products will be costed to the cent. After C4D engineers, architects, draughtsmen and women have designed the models we then pitch these up with UNDP, EU, UKAID, USAID and many other progressive institutions, individuals locally and globally. Development actors will pick on what responds to their missions, visions and respective Country Strategy for Zimbabwe. C4D legal, policy administrative Technical Working Groups will prioritize perfecting the models in the first 2 years before accelerated replication in order to have achieved substantial levels of sustainable development in the country by 2030.

GUIDING PRINCIPLES

C4D will

Collaborate with honest and trustworthy people to drive the development agenda for Zimbabwe.

Bring together all people from different walks of life interested. It is therefore envisaged that this development agenda pursued by C4D will be driven by development managers and practitioners.

DESCRIPTION OF MODELS

Fragmented and isolated interventions will not help much with holistic development needed in Zimbabwe. Whilst a certain measure of food security and nutrition targets and indicators can be reached through food aid distributions, school feeding schemes or any other piecemeal initiative, where there is no clean water, sanitation and affordable energy at household and community levels gains of the interventions will be eroded. C4D intends to ensure that existing structures at any level are thoroughly evaluated in order to BUILD BACK BETTER around those systems and infrastructures infused with modernized and sustainable technology. Where new systems and infrastructural developments are to be erected, constructed or established that’s the purpose for C4D technical working groups. Feasibility Study Teams and engineers should look at the big picture; For example, two rural geo-locations of Gokwe North and Chibi-Mwenezi come into mind. How can Tokwe-Mukosi dam complimented by Manyuchi dam be fully utilized to agriculturally transform the semi-arid districts of the lowveld including Chiredzi, Triangle and Chikombedzi.
There is huge economic potential undermined by political gamesmanship. The railway and road infrastructure from inland Zimbabwe to Maputo port could be modernized. Tourism connecting the Gonarezhou with Limpopo TransFrontier Parks of Kruger and Banhine-Limpopo in RSA and Mozambique is a game-changer. Close to 100 000 households could be transformed by C4D concept. Juxtaposition the above with Gokwe North Rural which literally is the breadbasket for the Midlands but has no roads, no water, no electricity connection, not even a technical college, but dysfunctional resettlement scheme, underutilized cotton ginnery with the other company known as GMB because Copper Queen Mine was syphoned dry, Sengwa Coal Mine went under with the frustration that visited RioZim. Wild animals kill more people’s livelihoods than droughts. Children drop out of school in preference for artisanal mining because their role models brothers and sisters are machete wielding champions hoping from one treacherous disused mine shaft to other. If we were to build Chipinge Chimanimani back better what will be involved? How about mining towns and cities littered across the country? Better qualified technocrats will have a better say than most of us.
